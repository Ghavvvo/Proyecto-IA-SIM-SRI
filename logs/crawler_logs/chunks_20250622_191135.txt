This esteemed institution is divided into two buildings, each showcasing a distinct facet of Cuban artistry. The Cuban Art Building boasts an extensive collection of works by renowned artists such as Wifredo Lam and Amelia Peláez, spanning from the colonial era to the present day. Meanwhile, the International Art Building features a diverse array of European and Latin American masterpieces, offering a global perspective on artistic movements throughout history. 2. Museo Nacional de la Lucha Contra Bandidos (National Museum of the Fight Against Bandits) Nestled within the cobblestone streets of Trinidad, the Museo Nacional de la Lucha Contra Bandidos stands as a testament to Cuba’s revolutionary struggle. Here, visitors can explore exhibits documenting the heroic efforts of ordinary Cubans who fought against counter-revolutionary forces during the tumultuous years following the 1959 Revolution. Artifacts, photographs, and personal accounts shed light on the sacrifices made in the name of freedom, making this museum a poignant reminder of Cuba’s resilience and determination. 3. Además de estas atrayentes actividades, podrás hacer rutas en kayak o en bicicleta acuática. Hasta Levisa solo se puede llegar en barcos que salen de la costa norte de Pinar del Río .
